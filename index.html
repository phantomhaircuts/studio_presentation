Types of Attacks

Q. What is SQL injection?
A. A SQL injection attack makes use of vulnerable user inputs in an application, and sends a malicious payload via SQL query. Attacks can potentially get user credentials, drop or delete a database, alter or add data etc. Q. What is a DDoS Attack?
A. A DoS or Denial of Service attack, is when an attacker renders a service unusuable and inaccessible by indefinitely disrupting services of a host. This attack is accomplished by flooding a target machine or resource with requests until it overloads the systems. A Distributed denial of service attack, distributes the incoming flood of traffice from multiple sources making it impossible to prevent the attack from simply blocking a single source.
Q. What is HTTP hacking?
A. Cross Site forgery attacks happen when a hacker sends a forged HTTP request to collect cookie information. Once the user’s session is compromised the hacker can generate requests to application identically to the user.
Q. What is a Phishing Attack
A. the fraudulent practice of sending emails purporting to be from reputable companies in order to induce individuals to reveal personal information, such as passwords and credit card numbers.

    • Roles:

    # Black hat

    The goal of black hat is to hack user’s devices and gain access. During the night, black hats wake up and together decide which players they want to attack using gestures and hand movements. At this point, only the black hats know who they are, and must actively try and avoid suspicion during the day. Black hats must hack all the users before being discovered, in order to win.

    White hat

    The goal of the white hat is to help users identify the black hats and potential attacks. White hats are given the opportunity to investigate one player each night. The moderator reveals the player to be a black hat with a thumbs down and similarly, a thumbs up reveals the player to be a user. A sideways thumb means that the player is either a phisher or a grey hat.

    Grey hat

    Grey hat can choose to save or kill one player each night. They can not save and kill at the same time. He/She can only save once and kill once in one game setting. They have the freedom to decide whose side they want to play on.

    Phisher

    Phisher will be able to choose any player, blindly, to swap identities with. After the first round, the Phisher must open their eyes along with the group they swapped with. They must act against the group whose card they receive. User

    User

    Normal Internet user that should protect themselves from being hacked by black hats or potentially grey hats. During the day users, unaware of who is playing what roles, get to discuss and then vote on who they think is a black hat. This person, regardless of their role will be kicked out of the game. If Users discover all the black hats before the black hats hack the users, they win.
